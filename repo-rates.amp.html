Repo Rate — Quick Guide

Updated: 30 September 2025

The repo rate is the rate at which the Reserve Bank of India lends short-term funds to commercial banks. It is a key monetary policy tool used to manage liquidity and inflation.

Why it matters

  • Higher repo rate → higher borrowing costs for banks → can lead to higher loan rates for borrowers.
  • Lower repo rate → cheaper bank borrowing → can result in lower lending rates and EMIs.

Where to check

  • Reserve Bank of India
